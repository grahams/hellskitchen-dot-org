Random Fact:
Starting 20 May, 325AD, the Church Fathers began to decide what was and wasn't canonical literature. It wasn't until the Council of Florence in 1439-1443 AD that the Pope issued a bull stating what were to be the officially recognized books of the New Testament. Several hundred other books that had been recognized as divinely inspired were left to rot in closets after that time.
