I'm glad things are in the past. That way no one can change them. No one can take them from me.
    -paraphrased from Ayn Rand, The Fountainhead
Hell's Kitchen was concieved of by Sean Hammond and Kelly Gunter in early 1995 shortly after they began printing Gracies Dinnertime Theatre. Thought of as a "satire exchange," the two attempted to contact college based satire publications on the web to make their pitch. No one replied.

     Over the next year Gracies Dinnertime Theatre divided, and divided again. By 1996 a total of three associated publications were located in Rochester and printing together. In time, it was recognized that an umbrella organization was needed.

     The name Hell's Kitchen was chosen for a number of reasons, mostly pertaining to in-group references:

  • Gracies Dinnertime Theatre often made reference to a fictional organization named Hell Inc. which had many subsideraries.
  • All the sister groups had titles dealing with food (Gracies Dinnertime Theatre, Melancholy Predator, 10:1 Cereal Delusions).
  • Hell's Kitchen, NYC was the stomping grounds of a number of authors, as well as the fictional location of the Wyland Building in Ayn Rand's Fountainhead.
     The list goes on, but I won't.

     As other groups joined Hell's Kitchen, the nature of the organization slowly changed until it became what it was first envisioned as: a story swap organization working to help small organizations reach larger audiences.
